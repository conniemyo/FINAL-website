Plan Ahead and Book During Sales!

The key to a budget-friendly adventure begins with a bit of planning and strategic booking. Planning your trips well in advance allows you to take advantage of early bird discounts and sales that many overlook. Not only can you usually guarantee a cheaper deposit, but most travel package providers allow you to secure a gradual payment plan to help ease the financial burden when scheduling so far in advance. I’ve always made it my mission to plan at a minimum of 6 months ahead of any trip I'm taking– whether it be a couple states away or a couple countries away. I would look into planning your vacation departures during an “off-season” as well, especially if you're looking into some of the more popular tourist attractions being offered. By arriving during the low-season for whatever your destination spot is, there's a much higher likelihood that you’ll be able to secure a spot to any sorts of activities or popular experiences as the number of visitors will be much lower. Planning ahead is an almost guaranteed way to provide yourself with a more relaxed and authentic experience as you not only avoid the crowded airports and tourist hotspots, but also save yourself from the financial worry of it all.

Dive through Croatia's Blue Cave

Nestled along the rugged coastline of Croatia's Adriatic Sea, the Blue Cave is a natural wonder that beckons adventurers from around the world. Accessible only by boat, this enchanting sea cave boasts mesmerizing azure waters illuminated by sunlight shining through an underwater opening where you can experience the cave's ethereal beauty firsthand. The Blue Cave is truly a geological marvel, and many adventure-goers on travel review sites have even gone as far as to describe the views inside as “otherwordly”. So if you ever find yourself in Croatia, make sure you don’t miss out on taking at least one speedboat tour through this gorgeous spot.

Take A Romantic Sojourn in Lisbon

If you’re in search of a city overflowing with romance and charm, Lisbon, Portugal, is the perfect destination. Start your journey with a short trip to the nearby wine towns, where the rolling vineyards await. The famous Sintra tour will take you around the more famous attractions that the city has to offer, with some travel sites offering them for only around a hundred dollars. The tour not only educates about the winemaking process but also offers an exciting lunch and wine tasting to enjoy with your partner. If you two are looking to create your own adventures, feel free to explore Sintra without the tour. This fairy-tale town offers more than enough enchanting castles and lush gardens for you and your partner to get lost in. As the day winds down, head to one of Lisbon's famous viewpoints to witness a breathtaking sunset over the Tagus River. The combination of wine, history, and scenic beauty guarantees a Valentine's Day that the both of you will remember for years to come.

Opt for Hostels and Utilize Credit Card Points for Flights

Accommodations and flights are going to be our two major contributors to extensive travel expenses. To cut costs on both fronts, I would consider opting to stay in hostels over the more traditional hotel option. Hostels are not only more budget-friendly but also offer a unique opportunity to connect with fellow travelers. It’s a much more community-centric option as you typically reside in a shared space with other travelers for the duration of your trip. Perfect if you’re a solo traveler looking to meet some unique people, or even a group of friends just hoping to share the experiences. A personal favorite travel tip of mine is saving up credit card points to utilize in my trips. Credit card points are a game-changer when it comes to booking flights, especially if you're a big spender throughout the year. Those points slowly but surely add up over the course of months or even years as you await your trip and eventually get to reap your rewards in the form of lower booking fees, cheaper flights or even a few free nights at your hotel. Many credit card companies even provide loyalty programs that allow you to redeem points for free gifts, free checked bags or even priority boarding. I would be sure to look into your current bank's travel point options and explore the credit card programs that align with your ideal travel goals. By strategically using your points, you can significantly reduce your airfare costs and allow yourself to spend a little extra cash on the more enjoyable aspects of your trip.
